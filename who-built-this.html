I

What You'll Walk Away With

By the end, you won't just know your character. You'll have excavated their complete psychological architecture.

Your Complete Character Architecture

The Surface Pattern — The behavior visible on the page. Observable, recurring, consequential.

The Hidden Truth — The belief operating beneath your character's awareness that makes their behavior feel necessary.

The Formative Wound — The specific experience that made that belief the only reasonable conclusion.

The Lie — The distorted conclusion your character drew from what happened, now shaping every choice they make.

Want vs. Need — What they're chasing, and what they actually require. Why those two things are in conflict, and why that conflict is your story.

You'll have a character who surprises you, resists easy resolution, and earns the reader's emotional investment — because they were discovered, not constructed.
