The Methodology

What makes STRATUM different from other character development courses? +
STRATUM teaches you to excavate rather than fabricate character psychology. Instead of filling out worksheets or inventing traits, you discover the psychological architecture that already exists in your intuitive understanding of your character. Using AI as a Socratic coaching partner, you systematically uncover behavior patterns, driving beliefs, formative wounds, and the complete causal chain that makes your characters psychologically coherent.
I've tried character worksheets before. Why didn't they work? +
Traditional worksheets ask you to invent disconnected facts: favorite color, biggest fear, childhood memory. These details don't add up to a coherent psychology. STRATUM works differently — you start with one observable behavior, then excavate the hidden truth, the formative wound, and the lie your character believes. Each element causally connects to the next. The result isn't a collection of facts. It's an integrated psychological architecture.
Do I need to understand psychology to use this method? +
No. You need to understand human behavior as a storyteller, not as a therapist. STRATUM borrows concepts from Cognitive Behavioral Therapy and Schema Therapy, but only to enrich your storytelling. You're not diagnosing. You're discovering what your character would have to believe for their behavior to make sense.

The Course Structure

How long does the course take? +
Most writers will complete one lesson per week, finishing the full course in approximately two months. Each lesson includes video instruction, an AI coaching session (35–40 minutes), and workspace exercises. You work at your own pace.
Do I need to complete the course in order? +
Yes. The excavation methodology is deliberately sequential. Lesson 3 (The Formative Wound) requires the hidden truth you discovered in Lesson 2. Lesson 4 (The Lie) emerges from the wound in Lesson 3. Skipping ahead disrupts the causal chain that creates psychological coherence.
Can I use this method for multiple characters? +
Absolutely. You'll initially select one character — typically your protagonist — to excavate. Once you've mastered the method, you can repeat the process for antagonists, supporting characters, or multiple POV characters. Most writers find the second excavation goes significantly faster.
