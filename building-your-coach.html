This course uses AI as your Socratic coaching partner, and you'll need an account with an AI platform. I use Claude, and I recommend it and use it in this course. It has a feature called Projects that gives you memory continuity across all lessons. That means your coach remembers your character, your anchor behavior, your hidden truth, and everything you've built, every time you return.

If you use a different system — Gemini, ChatGPT, Copilot — the lesson prompts will still work. You may have to paste in your previous session summary at the start of each chat to give context. The setup steps here are for Claude only.

Step 1 — Create Your Account.

Go to claude.ai. Create a free account, or sign in if you already have one. Claude offers a free tier and a paid plan called Pro. The free tier works for this course. Pro gives you higher usage limits, which you may want if you're working through lessons quickly. That decision is yours.

Step 2 — Create A Project.

Once you're logged in, look for Projects in the left sidebar. Click it, then click New Project. Name it something clear — STRATUM Character Development works well. This Project is the container for your entire course experience. Every lesson session you run will live here, and Claude will carry context from one session to the next.

Step 3 — Add Project Instructions.

Inside your Project, you'll see a section called Project Instructions. This is where you tell Claude its role for the entire course. Click to edit it and paste in the following prompt. I have provided a link below for you to easily copy and paste.

You are an expert writing coach specializing in character development using the STRATUM excavation methodology. You are a Socratic questioner, not a content generator. Your role is to help me discover what I already intuitively know about my character — never to generate content for me. You have memory of our previous sessions and should refer to prior work when relevant.

This instruction stays active for every session. You won't need to re-enter it.

Step 4 — Upload Your Course Files.

Inside your Project, you can upload files that Claude can reference throughout the course. At minimum, upload any character notes, story outlines, or reference material you already have. As you complete each lesson, you can add your Reflection Journal entries here as well. This deepens Claude's context over time. To upload: look for the Add content or paperclip option within the Project. Supported formats include PDF, Word documents, and plain text.

Step 5 — Test Your Setup.

Open a new chat inside your Project — not from the main sidebar, but from within the Project itself. This ensures the session inherits your instructions and files. Type in: "What do you know about my character so far?" At this point, Claude will tell you it has your instructions but no character details yet. That's correct. If you've already uploaded files, it may reference those. Either way, you're ready.

Memory continuity is important. Each time you return for a new lesson, open a new chat inside the same Project. Claude will remember your anchor behavior from Lesson 1, your hidden truth from Lesson 2, and so on. You don't need to re-explain your character from scratch each session — though the lesson prompts do include a brief summary field to sharpen focus.

This is the core advantage of the Project setup. It's not just convenience. It allows the coaching to deepen progressively, exactly the way the methodology is designed.

That's the full setup. Account, Project, Instructions, Files. You are ready to give your coach some context.
